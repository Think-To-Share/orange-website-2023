When we plan on how to describe a character in a children’s book, the foremost thing that is to be considered is using language that is appropriate for their age and reading level. While describing characters in a way that children can understand, ensure making sure the use of complex words or phrases that children might not understand to be avoided. It shall be wise to stick to words that are easy to read and comprehend. Description of the character’s physical features, such as hair color, eye color, and height help the children in better understanding- they tend to be very observant of people’s physical traits and easily relate to these descriptions. Description of the character’s behavior, actions, and thoughts help children in understanding the character’s personality and how they interact with other characters in the story. Compare the character to things that children are familiar with, such as animals or other characters from other books they may have read. Allow the character to speak for themselves thereby helping children in understanding the character’s personality and how they communicate with others. Include illustrations of the character to the extent possible in order to help children visualizing them and thereby helping in connecting with the character on a deeper level. It is important to be descriptive and concise simultaneously using language that is age-appropriate and engaging for children- as by doing that, one shall help children to better understand and connect with the characters in the book. On deciding on how to describe characters in a story, it is important to keep in mind that describing characters in a story is the most important part of creating a detailed and engaging narrative. Description of the character’s physical features such as their height, weight, hair color, eye color, facial features, clothing style, and any distinguishing marks or scars is important for a children book. Like, “David was a tall, muscular man with short blonde hair, piercing blue eyes, and a scar above his left eyebrow. He wore a black leather jacket and dark jeans.” The writer also need to use words to describe the character’s personality, including their strengths, weaknesses, quirks, and habits-like “Veronica was a kind-hearted person who always put others’ needs before her own. She was known for her unwavering loyalty to her friends and her infectious laugh.” Using dialogue to reveal a character’s personality and mannerisms in the way a character speaks can give the reader insights into their background, education, social status, and emotions. Like-‘”I’m sorry,” said Ruth softly, twirling a strand of her curly hair around her finger. “I didn’t mean to upset you. I just wanted to make sure you were okay.”’ Giving the reader information about the character’s past, including their family history, childhood, education, and experiences that have shaped who they are today is also important to keep the young reader glued to writing- like “Growing up, Harry was always fascinated by airplanes. His father was a pilot in the Air Force, and Tom spent countless hours tinkering with model planes in his bedroom. When he graduated high school, he enrolled in flight school and earned his pilot’s license.”
